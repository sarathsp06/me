<SectionHeading icon="fa-mountain" color="emerald">Lake Lucerne: The Main Base</SectionHeading>

<div class="prose-content">

Five nights at Lake Lucerne was the core of the trip. The plan was to pick a lakeside village — Weggis, Vitznau, or Emmetten — and use it as a base for everything: the Rigi day trip, the Lucerne city half-day, and two unhurried days of lake and village life.

</div>

<SectionHeading icon="fa-triangle-exclamation" color="red">Things to Remember</SectionHeading>

<div class="space-y-3">
  <PitfallCard title="Swiss motorway vignette">
    <p class="text-sm text-gray-600">Required for driving on Swiss motorways. Buy it before crossing the border (available at border petrol stations or online). Valid for the calendar year.</p>
  </PitfallCard>

  <PitfallCard title="Rigi summit is cold">
    <p class="text-sm text-gray-600">Even in April with pleasant lakeside weather, the summit sits around 8°C. Bring proper layers and gloves, especially for kids. Wind makes it feel colder.</p>
  </PitfallCard>

  <PitfallCard title="Rest stops with playgrounds">
    <p class="text-sm text-gray-600">Plan mid-drive breaks at rest stops with playgrounds — they exist along German motorways and make a huge difference with kids. Build an extra 30 minutes into each drive estimate.</p>
  </PitfallCard>
</div>

<Callout type="tip" title="Currency" icon="fa-wallet">
  <p class="text-sm text-gray-600">Switzerland uses CHF, not EUR. Bring some of both. Cards are widely accepted, but smaller villages and parking meters sometimes need cash.</p>
</Callout>
