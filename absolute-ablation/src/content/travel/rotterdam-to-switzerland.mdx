<SectionHeading icon="fa-route" color="blue">The Route</SectionHeading>

<div class="prose-content">

Our route takes us south from Rotterdam through Germany, looping into Switzerland for five nights at Lake Lucerne, then back north through quiet German villages — Kaiserstuhl wine country near Freiburg, then Rhine-side Königswinter near Bonn. Since I'm the only one driving, every leg stays under four hours, timed around Saanvi's nap schedule. Rest days at the main base. Ishani and Mum set the walking pace — no rushing, no steep hikes. On the return, scenic outskirts and river promenades instead of city centres.

  <StepItem number={8} title="Mount Pilatus — Golden Round Trip — Thu 1 May">
    <p class="text-sm text-gray-600 leading-relaxed"><strong>The big photo day. No driving.</strong> This is the one I've been dreaming about — the "golden round trip," a famous circular route combining boat, cable car, and the world's steepest cogwheel railway. Start: take the lake steamer from our village to Alpnachstad (~90 min, gorgeous ride). From Alpnachstad, ride the cogwheel railway to Pilatus Kulm summit (2,132m). Panoramic views of Alps and lakes — the most photogenic moment of the trip. The summit terrace is flat and fenced (safe with Ishani, and she'll be wide-eyed). Mum can sit at the summit restaurant and soak in the view. Descend by gondola and aerial cableway to Kriens, then bus or short taxi back to the Lucerne boat pier for the steamer home.</p>
    <p class="text-sm text-gray-600 leading-relaxed mt-2"><strong>Note:</strong> The cogwheel railway (Alpnachstad side) typically opens in late April/early May — check the Pilatus website for the exact 2025 opening date. If it's not yet running, we'll take the gondola from Kriens up and back instead (runs year-round). Strollers can board the boats and gondolas. The cogwheel carriages are tighter — our compact folding stroller should fit. My partner can stay at the village with Saanvi if the full loop feels too long for a 1-year-old.</p>
